# AEA's path beyond BoomPower

## Executive Summary

- **Do not clone either competitor.** BoomPower validates the guided home-electrification and managed-installer model. ServiceM8 defines the operational baseline trades now expect. AEA's opportunity is to join those two models while keeping customer identity, contact details and exact location outside trade and wholesaler accounts.
- **Make trust a product system, not only a promise.** AEA should retain customer-owned project history, explicit consent receipts, selective opportunity disclosure, structured quote responses, owner-scoped business records and exportable histories. This can be more defensible than a conventional lead marketplace or direct-contact CRM.
- **Use the trade operating portal as the onboarding engine.** A useful free operations foundation gives businesses a reason to set up before they pay. Commercial access should unlock platform leads, product visibility, platform conversion and expanded capacity. Admin-assigned premium features should remain separate.
- **The first foundation is now defined.** Business Hub starts with five free internal work records, member-expanded capacity, privacy-safe conversion of eligible installer opportunities or wholesaler product requests, schedules, stages, checklists, activity history and separately controlled team assignment.

## The market is split between journey orchestration and business operations

BoomPower publicly presents a three-sided system for households, enterprise program owners and installers. Its household experience includes whole-home modelling, upgrade recommendations, live price estimates, finance and installer selection. Its enterprise layer manages program journeys and installer quality. Its installer portal tracks interactions, performance, pricing, accreditation and product checks. This is strong evidence that AEA's ecosystem direction is commercially relevant, but it is not public evidence of a complete day-to-day field-service operating system.

ServiceM8 approaches the problem from the opposite direction. It starts with job cards, schedules, quotes, invoices, payments, client history, online booking and accounting integrations. Higher plans add asset management, electronic forms, proposals, inbox workflows, job costing and a knowledge base. Its product is operationally deep, but its standard model assumes the trade can receive and use customer contact information directly.

AEA should therefore occupy the space between them:

1. A free, persistent household planning account.
2. A verified installer and wholesaler marketplace.
3. A protected opportunity and response workflow without customer contact disclosure.
4. A role-aware business operating system for installers and wholesalers.
5. An enterprise and administration layer for approvals, service quality, complaints and program governance.

## What BoomPower does well

### It treats electrification as a multi-year journey

The public product is organised around a household pathway rather than a one-off product lead. This is the right model for AEA. Households rarely electrify everything at once, so project history, priorities, sequencing, finance readiness and completed work should persist over time.

### It puts installer quality inside the platform

BoomPower describes checks covering licences, insurance, business stability, ethical sales, customer satisfaction and product quality. It also describes complaint monitoring and possible installer removal. AEA should keep verification, approvals, complaint evidence, expiry monitoring and administrator action as platform functions rather than relying on badges alone.

### It uses enterprise partners as a distribution channel

Banks, retailers and government programs can sponsor or distribute household journeys. This creates a future B2B2C route for AEA, but the consumer product should remain independently useful without an enterprise sponsor.

### Its public privacy commitment is directionally strong

BoomPower says information is supplied only to suppliers or installers explicitly selected after a competitive process. AEA should go further by making the disclosure event, consent version, fields disclosed and withdrawal state visible to the customer.

## Where AEA can outperform BoomPower

### Customer data continuity and portability

BoomPower's public terms say it does not assure that submitted data will remain available over time. AEA should promise a durable customer project record, clear archive rules, downloadable project summaries and an account deletion path. This turns persistence into part of the trust proposition.

### No lead-list economics

AEA should continue to avoid selling customer contact records, selling individual leads, allowing wholesaler lead access or using paid placement to determine suitability. Installers should respond to anonymised scopes inside the platform. Wholesalers should receive only product and fulfilment workflows.

### Transparent product economics

Approved wholesaler products, stock, warranty, dependencies and trade prices can be selected by paid installers without turning the wholesaler into a household lead recipient. Over time, a quote should preserve separate product, labour, compliance, optional and warranty components.

### A complete operating layer

BoomPower's public material emphasises journey orchestration, installer quality and performance tracking. AEA can differentiate by providing the trade with the operational system needed after an opportunity becomes real, while still withholding household contact data.

## What ServiceM8 establishes as the operational baseline

ServiceM8's current Australian pricing shows a free solo plan with 30 jobs per month and paid tiers from $29 to $349 per month. All plans include job cards, scheduling, quotes, invoices, communication, card payments, online bookings, accounting integrations and reporting. Growing and higher plans add assets, custom electronic forms, proposals and other advanced workflows. Premium and higher plans add job costing, markup billing and knowledge tools.

This shows that a credible trade portal eventually needs:

- Work records, status stages and searchable history.
- Scheduling, crew allocation and notifications.
- Checklists, photos, documents, signatures and compliance forms.
- Product, labour and cost inputs.
- Quote, invoice, deposit and payment states.
- Asset, warranty and recurring maintenance history.
- Team roles, time tracking and an activity log.
- Accounting, calendar, email and storage integrations.
- Mobile and reduced-connectivity support.
- Business-owned exports, backups and security activity.

AEA should not copy ServiceM8's direct-contact client card, click-to-call, SMS marketing, live-arrival tracking or public online-booking model. Those features conflict with the platform's safety proposition unless redesigned as consent-controlled, platform-mediated interactions.

## Free, paid and administrator-assigned access

### Customer accounts

Customer accounts remain free with no project-count paywall. Planning, saved projects, private notes, project history, consent controls, quote comparison and document storage should remain free. Future revenue should come from trade memberships, enterprise programs and optional service layers, not from selling household data.

### Installer free account

- Business profile and coverage.
- Verification preparation and private evidence upload.
- Availability and account preferences.
- Five active internal Business Hub records.
- Basic stages, dates, checklists and owner-only activity.
- No household leads.
- No wholesaler product marketplace.
- No platform-work conversion.

### Installer paid membership

- Eligible anonymised household opportunities.
- Structured platform quote responses.
- Approved wholesaler product marketplace.
- Expanded Business Hub capacity.
- Conversion of eligible platform opportunities into protected work records.
- Future compliance, asset, warranty and integration features as they become production-ready.

### Wholesaler free account

- Business profile and verification preparation.
- Draft product creation.
- Five active internal fulfilment records.
- Basic stages, dates, checklists and owner-only activity.
- No household leads under any plan.
- No visibility inside installer product selection.
- No bulk catalogue workflow.
- No platform product-request conversion.

### Wholesaler paid membership

- Visibility for approved and published products.
- Bulk catalogue tools.
- Expanded fulfilment capacity.
- Conversion of installer product requests into fulfilment records.
- Future stock, order, warranty and service-level workflows.

### Administrator-assigned premium features

- Team access and shared ownership.
- Advanced analytics.
- Featured placement, subject to suitability and review controls.
- Priority support.
- Time-limited or permanent access overrides for individual accounts.

The administrator grant system should remain feature-specific, expiry-aware and auditable. A paid membership should not automatically unlock premium controls.

## Product sequence

### Foundation release

- Durable owner-scoped work records for installers and wholesalers.
- Free five-record foundation and paid expanded access.
- Privacy-safe platform conversion.
- Role-specific stages.
- Scheduling dates, priorities and checklists.
- Activity history.
- Premium crew assignment.

### Delivered release: assets, evidence and handover

- Installed-product and serial-number records.
- Warranty start, expiry and document storage.
- Trade-specific completion checklists.
- Protected compliance, commissioning, warranty, manual, datasheet and installation evidence.
- Administrator approval before customer publication.
- Customer-visible handover packs without direct contact details.
- Always-free customer asset, warranty and document access.

### Delivered release: lifecycle schedules and safety notices

- Recurring service and warranty reminders.
- Installer-maintained service history without household contact access.
- Customer-controlled dashboard reminders and Google Calendar actions.
- Administrator-sourced product safety notices using category, brand or model matching.
- Private customer acknowledgement records and audited publication or withdrawal.

### Delivered release: private ownership and record corrections

- Private asset ownership transfer using expiring one-time codes stored only as secure hashes.
- Separate current and receiving household consent followed by administrator approval.
- Immediate removal of the former household's live documents and lifecycle access after an approved transfer.
- Immutable transfer events without exposing one household's identity to the other.
- Versioned one-field handover corrections with administrator review and conflict protection.
- Customer-visible correction history that retains every previously approved value.

### Delivered release: product identity and business integrations

- Product identifier normalization for serial, batch and warranty references.
- Serial-range and batch-aware product notice matching after supplier identifier quality improves.
- Google Calendar schedule sync with owner-controlled calendars.
- Google Drive document destinations for authorised business files.
- Gmail-based platform notices without disclosing customer contact addresses.
- Xero and MYOB accounting connection pathways.
- Stripe and Square payment connection pathways.

### Delivered release: installer CRM refinement

- Business-owned customer records with full addresses and no map dependency.
- System-generated, chronological job identifiers that cannot be edited.
- A focused Today view, separate jobs, schedule, customers, tasks, templates, reports and integrations.
- Reusable job templates that prefill job details and copy practical task checklists.
- Responsive two-row phone navigation without a hidden horizontal menu.
- Protected platform jobs remain redacted and separate from direct trade customers.
- Offline-first mobile foundation for iOS and Android field work.

### Delivered release: regulated forms and recurring operations

- Trade-aware pre-start, safety, completion, commissioning and service supporting forms.
- Immutable template snapshots and versions on every job form record.
- Clear boundaries stating that supporting records do not replace licences, permits, certificates, standards or scheme documents.
- Recurring CRM jobs generated from asset lifecycle schedules with saved checklist templates.
- Daily and manual due-job generation with one durable job per service plan and due date.
- System-generated job numbers and the existing AEA protected-customer boundary on every generated job.
- Field app sync summaries for assigned technical forms, ready for offline form editing in the next native release.

### Next release: dispatch, purchasing and native forms

- Offline field-form completion with conflict-safe draft and completion actions.
- Administrator-published recommended form versions after state and trade review.
- Calendar-aware dispatch, workload conflicts and crew capacity.
- Job costing, proposal templates and progress payment schedules after the parked rate-card research is complete.
- Wholesaler purchase orders, fulfilment milestones and warranty claims.

### Later release: enterprise programs and deeper automation

- Rate cards after trade categories, labour structures and compliance needs are fully researched.
- Quote templates, product and labour bundles, deposits and progress states.
- Job costing and margin analysis.
- Enterprise program dashboards, installer service levels and outcome reporting.
- Native app distribution, device notifications and tested conflict resolution after release accounts are connected.

## Visual and journey audit, July 16 2026

The public BoomPower platform uses generous white space, large lifestyle photography and simplified illustrated portal scenes. It explains the three-sided household, enterprise and installer model clearly, but the public imagery does not demonstrate a detailed field-service workflow. AEA should retain its stronger operational depth while matching that level of public clarity through a single platform overview and role-specific access boundaries.

ServiceM8 exposes far more operational detail. Its pricing page communicates a broad baseline of jobs, schedules, quotes, invoices, payments, recurring jobs, templates, forms, assets, routes, costing and knowledge features. The comparison is dense and horizontally demanding, which makes discovery harder for a small operator. AEA should group capability around the workday instead of exposing every tool at once.

The AEA signed-in desktop journey now has the right foundation: a calm Today view, separate work areas and a two-panel job view. The phone audit found that eight CRM destinations were previously presented as a horizontally scrolling strip, leaving important destinations easy to miss. The navigation is now a visible four-column, two-row control. Reusable templates also remove repeated data entry without making the new-job form longer.

The public access story is now explicit. Customers remain free, installer and wholesaler profiles are free to prepare, unpaid installers receive no household opportunities, unpaid wholesaler products remain invisible, and administrator-assigned premium controls are separate from paid membership. This clarity is important because the live AEA memberships bundle marketplace, catalogue and operating tools and therefore should not be marketed as universally cheaper than a standalone CRM.

## Recommended decision

Build offline field-form completion next, using the versioned form snapshots and sync summaries now in place. In parallel, add wholesaler purchase orders and fulfilment milestones so installer product selections can continue into supply operations without waiting for the parked rate-card assumptions. Complete the external release-account steps for Google OAuth, iOS registration and internal mobile builds.

## Caveats and evidence sources

- This review uses official public product, pricing, privacy, terms, support and complaint pages accessed on July 15, 2026. It does not include hands-on access to private enterprise portals or paid product accounts.
- BoomPower case-study outcomes and customer counts are company-reported and were not used as independent performance proof.
- Public feature pages describe marketed capability, not implementation quality, usage, uptime or customer satisfaction.
- Pricing and plan inclusions can change. Confirm them before any external sales comparison.
